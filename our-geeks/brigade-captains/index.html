Become a fellow

Over 600 people applied to be Code for America fellows for the 2014 program. Candidates came in from across the country and around the world, and they came with backgrounds ranging from back-end development experience to urban design.

Meet the 2014 fellows

  • Hans Thompson

    Anchorage, AK Brigade

    I’ve grown up in west Anchorage and still think Alaska is the best place to live after traveling around the US and nearly every state.

    I’ve been playing recently with crime map data, the annual finance reporting for municipalities, and the financial records of political campaigning. After the beginning of the recession, I needed to find a way to apply myself to understand the institutional misunderstandings that led to a bruising of the economy. Using R for analytics is one way for me to answer the questions that I’ve had.

  • Scott Barnwell

    Asheville, NC Brigade

    Scott Barnwell is an experienced GIS/IT professional who has worked in the public, private, and non-profit sectors for the past twenty years. He currently works for the City of Asheville managing a variety of IT projects and supporting GIS operations throughout the city. As a co-founder and co-captain of Asheville’s Code for America Brigade, Scott is helping to foster a local civic tech community that can focus energies on opening up access to public data and building value-added apps using that data.

    Asheville, NC Brigade

    Dave Michelson – Currently a GIS Analyst with The City of Asheville and co-captain of the Code for Asheville Brigade. A BIG advocate of using Open Source software, especially in local Government. 20 years of experience in GIS spanning several industries – mapping, consulting, County Government, Public Schools, and City Government. The majority of the time working with web mapping and Database Administration.

  • Chip Rosenthal

    Austin, TX Brigade

    Chip Rosenthal has been developing systems and tools based on open source software and open network protocols for over 20 years. He is the owner of Unicom Systems Development, a provider of technology consulting and contract development services.

    He currently serves on the Austin Community Technology and Telecommunications Commission, which advises the Austin City Council on technology matters. Also, he is the current chair for Open Austin, a volunteer group that advocates for open government, open data, and civic technology.

  • Harlan Weber

    Boston, MA Brigade

    Harlan Weber is a UX designer and Brigade Captain at Code for Boston. As a designer and researcher, Harlan has worked on a wide variety of projects ranging from web and mobile apps, to medical and industrial instruments, to product and brand strategies while working at OnForce, Intuit's Innovation Lab, and several Boston-area design firms.

    As Brigade Captain at Code for Boston, Harlan has helped to activate the volunteer civic hacking community in Boston through developer outreach, partnerships with municipal and governmental groups, and running civic hack nights as part of the inaugural Brigade class.
