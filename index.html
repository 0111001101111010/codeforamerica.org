Code for America

Code for America is redoing our website.

For the past few months, we’ve worked with Colorado branding firm Dojo4, Brighton web design firm Clearleft, and our own internal communications and technology teams on a new visual design and front-end for the Code for America website. Today, we’re making the first of this work public and asking for input from the CfA community to ensure that the information presented on the site is accurate and up-to-date.

See The Site | Who’s Working On It | Help Us Fix Things

A possible look for our new home page, as of Thursday, January 16:

See The Site

  • About: information on staff and programs
  • Governments: current and past partners
  • Citizens: brigades, fellowship, and other geeks
  • Apps: accelerator, incubator, and fellowship apps
  • Support Our Work: how to donate
  • Blog: words
  • Library: under construction back-catalog of videos, etc.

    • Help Us Fix Things

    The new website is hosted statically, and you can help us fix out-of-date information by using a pull request to the Github repository, codeforamerica/codeforamerica.org. The only code skill required is HTML markup.

    For members of the community including fellows, brigade captains, and staff, find yourself and your friends in the big list of people or one of the lists of civic hackers and let us know if your bio is correct and photo current.

    If you built an app or project as part of the fellowship or accelerator programs, find it on the apps page and verify that we’ve got it described accurately.

    Finally, check government information for brigades and fellowships on the 2014 government partners and alumni government partners pages and fill in any blanks you might find.
