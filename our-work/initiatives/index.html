Code for America's major initiatives are collections of work on significant civic technology challenges. The results may include everything from software to policy.

Digital Front Door

A critical interaction point

Cities' main websites are the online equivalent of city halls. Residents expect to be able to go there to do all of their government business in one place. For local government staff, providing this is easier said than done. Code for America is working with several US cities to rethink the city website from both sides.

Health & Human Services

Serving Vulnerable Residents

Our efforts have included software to make it easier for people to sign up for food stamps, data standards for restaurant and home safety inspections, and policy changes to allow governments to communicate with clients via SMS.

Procurement

The Foundation for Change

Government purchasing rules almost make it inevitable that local governments will be poorly served by their technology vendors. We are working to shift the dynamic, pilot new models, and bring new vendors into the ecosystem.
